# Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

TabSnap is a Chrome extension that saves and restores your browser tabs and windows.

## Information We Collect

TabSnap handles the following information to provide its core features:

- Open tab URLs.
- Tab state, including whether a tab is pinned or active.
- Window state and tab ordering.
- Snapshot timestamps.

## How We Use Information

We use this information only to:

- Save snapshots of your open tabs and windows.
- Restore saved snapshots later.
- Display your saved snapshot list in the extension popup.

## How Information Is Stored

All snapshot data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's local storage.

- We do not send your data to any external server.
- We do not sell, share, or transmit your data to third parties.
- Your snapshot data stays in your browser unless you delete it.

## Third Parties

TabSnap does not use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services.

## Incognito Mode

TabSnap does not intentionally store data from Incognito windows.

## Your Choices

You can:

- Create new snapshots.
- Delete saved snapshots from the extension popup.
- Remove all TabSnap data by clearing the extension's local storage or uninstalling the extension.

## Data Security

Because TabSnap stores data locally in your browser, you should treat your browser profile as private. While TabSnap does not transmit data to a server, locally stored browser data may still be accessible to anyone with access to your device or browser profile.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted here with a new "Last updated" date.
